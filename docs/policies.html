Staff members are expected to follow these policies and instructions.

How to contact other staff:

 Simply send mail to staff@. All staff will receive mail sent to staff@.

How to host open shop:
  1. arrive on time to unlock.
  2. put out the sidewalk sign
  3. put out the steel sign in front of the garage
  4. at the end of open shop:
    • put away the tools.
  5. lock up.

If you can't make your scheduled shift, please notify staff@ soon so we can find somebody to cover.

Buying Things

 If you have identified a need for a bike tool, promotional material, etc., follow these instructions:
  1. Send an email to staff@ stating your request.
  2. Use your best judgement to determine consensus.
  3. Make the purchase, and keep the receipt.
  4. Contact Luis for reimbursement.

Organizing Events

Events increase popularity and, indirectly, membership. They facilitate interaction between members of the local cycling community. Generally, there are two types of events: social events and workshops. If you are trying to organize an SBK event, see instructions below:

  1. Pick a day(s). Generally:
    • Monday nights are reserved for folk music
    • Last Wednesday nights the downstairs area is reserved for cryptoparty
    • Saturdays from 13:00-17:30 the upstairs area is reserved for BOSLAB
    • 4th Tuesday of the month from 18:30-21:30 the upstairs area is reserved for BOSLAB
  2. Notify Shaunalynn (part of the Sprout team) to make sure that there will be no schedule conflicts. Provide the date and time that we are planning to hold the event. E-mail shaunalynn _at_ thesprouts (dot) org
  3. Write short descriptions (short paragraph) for each session/day of the event.
  4. [optional] Create a flyer for the event. For help, contact Yurij, Luis, and Troy. EXAMPLE FLYER
  5. Add the event to the SBK website. Make a new event page for each session/day of the event. INSTRUCTIONS. Contact Troy for help.
  6. Send an announcement e-mail to the discuss@ mailing list. Aggregate the descriptions for all sessions/days of the event and send one e-mail. Touch on the following topics: introduction, rationale, sessions, rsvp request (usually informal), admission fee (usually free with suggested donation of $10), and any help needed from the community (e.g. we may need to borrow tools). Remember to attach the flyer, if there is one.
  7. [optional] Re-send the announcement e-mail to other mailing lists and internet communities as you see fit (do not include multiple lists in the same e-mail). Use your best judgement to determine which additional mailing lists will find this event relevant.
    • bcn _at_ lists (dot) bostoncoop (dot) net - post to the Boston Coop Network mailing list if it is a social event (such as bike-in movie).
    • bostonareafamilybike _at_ googlegroups (dot) com - post to the Boston Area Family Bikes mailing list if it is a social event (such as bike-in movie).
    • discuss _at_ artisansasylum (dot) com - post to the Artisans Asylum mailing list for events that makerspace and hacker folk may find interesting.
  8. [optional] For social events, notify the Somerville Bicycle Committee about the event so they can put it on their public calendar and distribute the event notification to all Somerville cyclists.
  9. [optional] Create a Facebook event. Remember to attach the flyer, if there is one. Contact Val for help.
  10. [optional] Create a twitter post. Remember to attach the flyer, if there is one. Contact Jenny for help (search your inbox for Jenny's e-mail address).
  11. If it will be a large, long-running social event (e.g. movie night), be sure to notify the neighbors of Sprout. Employ the rest of SBK staff to help. Finish this at least one day before the event!
